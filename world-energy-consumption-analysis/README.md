**World Energy Consumption Analysis**

**Project Overview**

This project analyses global energy consumption patterns from 2000 to 2022 across 180+ countries to identify structural shifts in energy demand, measure the pace of the fossil-to-renewable transition, and forecast future consumption using machine learning models.

**Methods Used**


Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Correlation Analysis
Feature Engineering
Linear Regression
Random Forest Regression


**Key Insights**

GDP shows the strongest positive relationship with energy consumption (r ≈ 0.83), confirming economic growth as the primary demand driver. Fossil fuels still account for ~80% of global supply despite renewables tripling over the study period. The Random Forest model achieved an R² of 0.97, validating its use for investment-grade energy demand forecasting.

**Outcome**

Random Forest outperforms Linear Regression significantly (R² 0.97 vs 0.88), capturing non-linear relationships between economic indicators and energy demand. Targeted policy in just 10 countries can influence over 65% of global energy outcomes.
